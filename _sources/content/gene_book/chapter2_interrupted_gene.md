This chapter focuses on gene structure, particularly the organization of exons and introns, and computational methods for analyzing interrupted genes.

## Gene Structure Analysis

### Exon-Intron Boundary Prediction

#### Splice Site Prediction Models
- **Description**: Computational models for predicting splice sites in interrupted genes
- **Input**: Genomic DNA sequences
- **Output**: Splice site locations and confidence scores
- **Application**: Gene annotation, alternative splicing analysis

### Intron Analysis

#### Intron Length and Content Analysis
- **Description**: Analysis of intron characteristics across species and gene types
- **Input**: Genomic sequences with annotated introns
- **Output**: Intron feature predictions
- **Application**: Evolutionary analysis, gene expression regulation

## Alternative Splicing

### Tissue-Specific Splicing

#### SpTransformer Applications
- **Description**: Application of SpTransformer to tissue-specific alternative splicing
- **Input**: Pre-mRNA sequences and tissue context
- **Output**: Tissue-specific isoform predictions
- **Application**: Understanding tissue-specific gene expression

### Splicing Regulatory Elements

#### Enhancer and Silencer Prediction
- **Description**: Prediction of splicing regulatory elements in introns and exons
- **Input**: Gene sequences with splicing annotations
- **Output**: Regulatory element locations
- **Application**: Understanding splicing regulation mechanisms
